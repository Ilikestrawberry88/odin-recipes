Pinnekjøtt

Description

Pinnekjøtt Pork chops - or lamb ribs - are the most common Christmas food in Western Norway, and have entered the competition as the most popular Christmas food across the country. The unique flavor comes from the old preservation methods; salting and drying. And you, if you're going to have really juicy pork chops, use the rule of thumb '30-3'. That means 30 hours of dilution and 3 hours of steaming. This is the rule that will give a juicy and good result, regardless.

Ingredients:

Makes five portions.

  • 2 kg salt-cured lamb ribs
Root vegetable mash:
  • 1.25 kg swedes (also known as rutabagas)
  • 2 carrots
  • 1 potato
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 100 ml cream
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp pepper

Steps:

  1. 1. You'll need approximately 400g lamb ribs per person. T he meat has to be cut before being left to soak in cold water. Completely submerge the meat, using plenty of cold water, and let the ribs rest at room temperature for about 30 hours. Remember to change the water once about half-way through this period.
  2. 2. Place a metal steamer rack or birch sticks (bark-free) at the bottom of a big pot (like a lobster pot). Add water until it reaches the rack or the sticks. Put the lamb ribs on top, before covering the pot. Bring the water to a boil, lower the heat and let the lamb ribs steam at 85-95 degrees Celsius for approximately 3 hours, or until the meat falls off the bone. Keep an eye out in case the water evaporates. There should always be water in the pot.
  3. 3. Peel the potato, carrots and swedes. Chop them into chunks and boil them until soft in lightly salted water. Drain the water and mash the vegetables.
  4. 4. Add butter and cream to the mash. Add salt and pepper to taste. Grated nutmeg can also be added for some extra zing.
Merry Christmas!
